# SoftwareDevelopmentLifecycle

# How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
I interpret user needs by focusing on what the user is trying to accomplish and breaking that down into smaller, clear requirements. Creating user stories helps a lot because they put the focus on the user’s perspective instead of just technical details. For example, writing something like “As a user, I want to search for trips so I can find the best option” makes it easier to understand the goal. From there, I can turn those stories into features and test cases, which helps make sure the program actually meets user expectations.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
I approach developing programs step by step by breaking big tasks into smaller pieces and working on them incrementally. Instead of trying to build everything at once, I focus on completing small parts and improving them over time. In the future, I want to continue using Agile practices like sprint planning, daily communication, and continuous testing. I also think backlog refinement and regular feedback are important because they help catch issues early and keep the project on track.

# What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member means communicating clearly, being open to feedback/constructive criticism , and helping others when needed. It’s important to stay involved throughout the process, not just focus on your own tasks. A good team member also takes responsibility for their work and makes sure it meets quality standards. In Agile teams, collaboration is key, so supporting teammates and sharing ideas helps the whole team succeed.
